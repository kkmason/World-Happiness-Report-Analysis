Happiness and Well-being

Introducing Positive Psychology to Policy Makers

Happiness is a state to which we all aspire to attain. Happiness is often viewed as a subjective, elusive, and intangible idea that varies from individual to individual; however, if we look at happiness from a collective perspective, we can find some common denominators that contribute to levels of happiness. Leading research in the field of Positive Psychology has identified a variety of factors, such as age or income, that are correlated to happiness. To find out more about the subject of happiness, the World Happiness Report was issued using data from Gallup poll. In this research project, we will be examining some common themes that underly happiness and identify ways in which these results can be incorporated into public policy. Using datasets from the World Happiness Report and the Gross National Happiness Index, we will look at how countries stack up against each other, factors that influence rankings, and what we, as Americans, can do to improve our own ranking.
